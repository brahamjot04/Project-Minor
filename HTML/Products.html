PUFF PASTRY

Puff pastry is a light, buttery, flaky dough used in both sweet and savory dishes, from appetizers to main courses to desserts. It contains only three ingredients—flour, butter, and salt—yet it rises to a great height with no added leavening agent.
The French perfected the technique but puff pastry dough appears in recipes from around the world.
Puff pastry resembles a croissant, with visible airy layers. Though both are laminated doughs, puff pastry does not include yeast or any other leavening ingredient.
It's not difficult to make puff pastry, but it's a painstaking process. It takes an elaborate rolling technique to form those hundreds of layers, and because the butter must be cold, it takes considerable pressure to roll the dough flat.
Also, the dough needs to chill between each round of rolling and folding. The effort is physically demanding and time-consuming.

MUFFINS & CUPCAKES

Cupcakes and muffins are single-serving baked goods with distinct textures, flavors, and baking processes.Learn about the differences between cupcakes and muffins.
Cupcakes are small cakes that feature a topping of whipped icing sugar, a candy garnish, or another decorative element. The name “cupcake” originated from the concept of baking miniature cakes in small cups.
A muffin is an individual-sized quick bread that rises using baking soda or baking powder instead of yeast. These small quick breads are usually sweet, with a denser texture than cupcakes.
Depending on the mix-in ingredients, you can enjoy muffins as a breakfast food, an accompaniment to a main course, or a snack.
